# Clinical Prose Guardrails

## Preferred wording

- "was associated with" for observational associations.
- "improved detection" only when the endpoint is detection.
- "clinical utility remains uncertain" when evidence is model performance or workflow-only.
- "absolute risk difference", "risk ratio", "hazard ratio", or "number needed to treat/harm" when available.

## Avoid

- "proves", "prevents death", or "improves outcomes" unless directly tested.
- "real-world ready" without deployment evidence.
- "unbiased" or "generalizable" without external validation across relevant populations.
- Hiding harms, missingness, multiplicity, or protocol deviations.
